<p><p>The automotive damper and shock absorber market comprises two primary types: hydraulic and pneumatic. Hydraulic dampers utilize fluid to control oscillations and minimize vibrations in vehicle suspension, providing a smooth ride. They are commonly found in standard vehicles due to their effective performance and reliability. Pneumatic dampers, on the other hand, use air pressure to absorb shocks, offering adjustable stiffness and improved comfort. This type is often used in high-end or commercial vehicles for enhanced load-bearing capacity and ride quality.</p></p>
