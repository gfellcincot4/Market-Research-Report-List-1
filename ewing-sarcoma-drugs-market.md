<p><p>The Ewing Sarcoma drugs market mainly comprises chemotherapeutic agents such as Vincristine, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, Etoposide, Ifosfamide, and Dactinomycin. Vincristine disrupts cancer cell division, while Cyclophosphamide damages DNA to inhibit growth. Doxorubicin, an anthracycline, intercalates DNA to interfere with replication, and Etoposide inhibits topoisomerase, preventing DNA repair. Ifosfamide functions similarly to Cyclophosphamide, causing DNA damage, and Dactinomycin binds to DNA, impeding transcription. Together, these drugs form a cornerstone in the treatment of Ewing Sarcoma.</p></p>
