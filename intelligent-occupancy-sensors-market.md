<p><p>Intelligent occupancy sensors can be categorized into wired and wireless networks based on their method of connectivity. Wired network sensors use physical cables to connect to a central control system, ensuring stable and secure communication. They are reliable in environments where power outages are less frequent but may require more installation effort. In contrast, wireless network sensors utilize radio frequency signals, allowing for flexible installation and scalability. They are ideal for retrofitting projects but may face challenges in signal interference or battery management.</p></p>
