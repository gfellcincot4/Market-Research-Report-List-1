<p><ul><li>Tethered</li><li>Self-floating</li><li>Cable</li><li>Sputnik</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The submarine seismograph market is categorized into four main types based on their deployment methods and features. Tethered seismographs are anchored to the seabed, providing stable data collection. Self-floating instruments drift on the ocean surface, capturing seismic activity without fixed installations. Cable seismographs link multiple sensors through underwater cables, ensuring real-time data transmission. Sputnik seismographs are innovative, autonomous units that operate independently in deep-sea environments, enhancing research capabilities by exploring remote oceanic regions for seismic events.</p></p>
